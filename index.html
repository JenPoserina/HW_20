They are on a mission to raise public awareness about PCOS. To help people with the condition overcome their symptoms and reduce their risk for life-threatening related diseases such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease and cancer.

Research

User Interviews

3 people were interviewed via Zoom. We asked participants about their interests and volunteer/donation habits, how they choose and engage with charities, and how they feel about their contributions.

Surveys

We recieved 12 Survey responses. We asked respondents about donation frequency, what qualities or actions they look for in charities, and the ways they contribute.

Insights

Users volunteer mostly in their local areas. Tthey prefer to see how their efforts are making a difference within their communities.

Trust is also a key motivator for involvement.

The most shocking insight was donation frequency, we did not expect to see any weekly responses and monthly responses were slightly higher than expected.

Problem Statement

PCOS Challenge was designed to be a nonprofit patient support and advocacy organization for women suffering polycystic ovarian syndrome. We have observed that although users may be familar with the illness and willing to get involved, many are not aware of the resources available through their local PCOS Challenge branch.

How might we help PCOS Challenge communicate their mission, build user trust, and increase donations while keeping patient resources at the forefront of the design?
